Coffee Menu

Latte $2.00

Lattes are usually considered to be an introductory coffee drink because their bitterness is cut by the amount of milk in the beverage.

Capuccino $2.20

Cappuccino is essentially a latte made with more foam than steamed milk and is often topped with a hit of cocoa powder or cinnamon.

Americano $3.00

Americanos are the go-to coffee for those seeking that caffeine kick during sluggish midday. This type of coffee is notoriously high in caffeine and has a flavour profile similar to black coffee.

Espresso $1.50

Who doesn’t love an espresso? This coffee drink is distinguished for its concentrated flavour, syrupy content and layer of foam.

Doppio $2.50

Simply known as the double espresso, Doppio means “double” in Italian explains the commonly called a standard double — so double or nothing.

Cortado $2.00

Like yin and yang, the cortado is the perfect balance of espresso and warm steamed milk.

Mocha $3.00

The mocha is a chocolate espresso drink with steamed milk and foam and is an exquisite mix of coffee and hot chocolate.

Red Eye $3.00

The red eye is an American type of coffee that combines brewed coffee with an espresso shot.

Frappuccino $3.50

Every millennial has heard of the Frappuccino. Its name is a portmanteau frappe and cappuccino and is a trademark brand of Starbucks.

Galão $2.00

This Portuguese coffee drink is similar to a latte & cappuccino and is almost synonymous with breakfast best enjoyed with hearty pastries.

Breve $3.00

The breve is a decadent twist to the average espresso.

Café au Lait $3.00

Pronounced “cafe oh ley”, is typically made with French press coffee instead of an espresso shot to bring out the different flavours.

Lungo $1.50

You may think that the lungo is another type of coffee, but it’s the new espresso.

Macchiato $2.00

The macchiato is another espresso-based coffee that’s topped with foam.

Ristretto $3.00

Ristretto is simply half of a shot of espresso. The result? A more concentrated coffee with an exquisite balance of elements than the regular espresso.

Affogato $2.50

This coffee is the only excuse for you to enjoy a scoop of ice cream during the day. Affogatos are more of a dessert coffee than a regular cup of coffee.

Irish $4.00

Hot coffee, sugar, whipped cream, and whiskey — that’s what Irish coffee is all about.

Nitro $2.25

Characteristically, nitro is something we wish to be — smooth, clean and cool.

Cold Brew $3.50

Cold brew coffee has become more and more popular over the past decade.

Vienna $2.75

Vienna is a type of coffee that’s traditional cream-based — and that’s a good thing for the palate.
